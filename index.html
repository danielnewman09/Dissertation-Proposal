Root Mean Square

$$\mbox{RMS} = \sqrt{\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x^2_i} $$
  • Provides information on intensity of vibration
  • As a bearing nears the end of its life, RMS value rapidly increases

Kurtosis

$$\mbox{Kurt} = \dfrac{\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n(x_i - \bar{x})^4}{\left ( \dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n(x_i - \bar{x})^2 \right )^2} - 3 $$
  • Provides information on impulsivity of vibration
  • As a signal degrades, its kurtosis increases.
  • A perfectly Gaussian signal will have a kurtosis of zero.

Spectral Kurtosis

  • When a bearing defect manifests, it may cause amplitude modulation at very high frequencies.
  • By analyzing the signal kurtosis at specific frequencies, it is possible to determine the bands in which this modulation occurs.

Background: Bearing Failure

Amplitude Demodulation

  • This is a signal processing technique by which a highly modulated signal may be manipulated to reveal an underlying defect.
  • The steps are as follows:
    1. Filter the signal about the modulating frequencies with a band-pass filter
    2. Rectify the filtered signal
    3. Use a low-pass filter to recover an envelope of the resulting signal
